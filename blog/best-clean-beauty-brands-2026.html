What Clean Beauty Actually Means in 2026

There is no FDA-regulated definition of "clean beauty." The term is a marketing designation adopted by brands and retailers to indicate products formulated without certain controversial ingredients. Most clean beauty brands exclude parabens, sulfates, phthalates, synthetic fragrances, formaldehyde and its releasers, mineral oil, coal tar, hydroquinone, triclosan, and certain chemical UV filters like oxybenzone.

Sephora's "Clean at Sephora" seal, launched in 2018 and updated in subsequent years, requires brands to be formulated without over 50 specific ingredients or ingredient categories. Credo Beauty and The Detox Market maintain their own, often stricter, clean standards. These retailer-driven standards have become the de facto industry benchmarks.

It is worth noting that "clean" does not mean "natural" or "organic." Clean beauty brands use both natural and synthetic ingredients, as long as those synthetics meet their safety standards. Some effective synthetic ingredients, like lab-produced hyaluronic acid and niacinamide, are staples of clean beauty formulations.

ILIA Beauty -- Best Overall Clean Brand

Overview

ILIA has established itself as the gold standard in clean beauty by consistently delivering products that perform at the level of conventional luxury brands. Founded in 2011, the brand has grown from a small lip care line into a full-range makeup brand available at Sephora, Nordstrom, and Credo Beauty. ILIA's formulations combine clean ingredients with proven actives like niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, and botanical extracts.

Standout Products

  • Super Serum Skin Tint SPF 40 ($48): The product that made ILIA famous. A lightweight tinted serum with SPF 40, hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, and squalane. Provides light-to-medium coverage with a natural, dewy finish. 30 shades. This is the clean beauty product most frequently cited as matching or exceeding conventional alternatives.
  • Limitless Lash Mascara ($28): A clean mascara that delivers length, volume, and hold without flaking. Formulated with shea butter, beeswax, and keratin. One of the few clean mascaras that competes with conventional favorites like Maybelline Lash Sensational.
  • Multi-Stick ($34): A cream stick for cheeks, lips, and eyes in 12 shades. Blends seamlessly and layers without creasing. Made with organic shea butter, avocado oil, and jojoba oil.

Why ILIA Leads

ILIA excels because it treats clean formulation as a baseline, not a selling point. The products are evaluated on performance first. The brand also demonstrates strong environmental commitment: packaging uses recycled aluminum, refillable components, and FSC-certified paper. B Corp certified since 2021.

Tower 28 Beauty -- Best for Sensitive Skin

Overview

Tower 28 was founded specifically for people with sensitive and reactive skin. Every product is tested by dermatologists and conforms to the National Eczema Association's ingredient standards, meaning the entire line is suitable for people with eczema, rosacea, and other skin conditions. The brand is also one of the most affordable in the clean beauty space.

Standout Products

  • ShineOn Lip Jelly ($15): A non-sticky, fragrance-free lip gloss in wearable sheer shades. The formula is so gentle it was the first lip gloss to earn the NEA Seal of Acceptance.
  • BeachPlease Tinted Lip + Cheek Balm ($20): A cream blush and lip color in one compact. Clean color that blends with fingers in seconds.
  • SunnyDays SPF 30 Tinted Sunscreen ($30): A mineral SPF tint with no white cast, in 16 shades. Provides sheer coverage and sun protection in one step.

Why Tower 28 Stands Out

Tower 28 proves that clean beauty does not have to be expensive. Most products fall under $25. The NEA certification provides a level of ingredient validation that goes beyond typical clean beauty standards. For anyone with sensitive skin who has struggled to find makeup that does not cause irritation, Tower 28 is the brand to try first.

Kosas -- Best Clean Makeup Performance

Kosas approaches clean beauty with a makeup-artist sensibility, creating products designed for buildable, blendable application that looks like skin, not makeup. The brand's tagline, "makeup for skincare freaks," reflects its dual focus on cosmetic performance and skin-nourishing ingredients.

Standout Products

  • Revealer Skin-Improving Foundation SPF 25 ($42): Medium-to-full coverage foundation with niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, and peptides. 28 shades with surprisingly accurate color matching. Sets naturally without powder.
  • Cloud Set Baked Setting and Smoothing Powder ($34): A talc-free setting powder that blurs pores and sets makeup without a cakey finish. Available in 8 flexible shades.
  • DreamLift Mascara ($26): Clean mascara with castor oil and peptides that conditions lashes while delivering buildable volume and curl.

Saie -- Best Clean Everyday Makeup

Saie focuses on everyday, no-fuss makeup that simplifies routines. Products are designed to go on fast, look natural, and last all day. The brand prioritizes multitasking products that reduce the number of steps and products in your routine.

Standout Products

  • Glowy Super Gel ($25): A lightweight luminous primer/highlighter that creates a natural lit-from-within glow. Apply before or after foundation, or wear alone on bare skin.
  • Slip Tint Dewy Tinted Moisturizer SPF 35 ($35): Sheer coverage with mineral SPF, hyaluronic acid, and licorice root extract. 18 shades. Takes 30 seconds to apply and looks like skin.
  • Dew Blush ($25): A liquid blush that blends seamlessly and gives a naturally flushed look. Available in 10 shades from subtle nude to vibrant berry.

Merit -- Best Minimalist Clean Brand

Merit embodies the less-is-more philosophy with a tightly curated product line designed to create a complete look with the fewest possible products. The brand's five-minute face concept resonated powerfully with consumers who want to look polished without spending 30 minutes on makeup.

Standout Products

  • The Minimalist Perfecting Complexion Stick ($38): A buildable cream foundation in stick form. Applies with fingers in under a minute. 20 shades with natural, skin-like coverage.
  • Flush Balm Cheek Color ($28): A cream blush in a twist-up stick. Six shades designed to look natural on all skin tones. Blends without tools.
  • Day Glow Highlighting Balm ($30): A subtle, non-glittery highlighter that creates a soft-focus glow without visible shimmer particles.

Westman Atelier -- Best Luxury Clean Beauty

Created by celebrity makeup artist Gucci Westman, Westman Atelier represents the luxury end of clean beauty. Products feature premium ingredients, sophisticated packaging (refillable compacts and magnetic palettes), and the kind of seamless application that reflects Westman's decades of professional experience.

Pricing and Value

Westman Atelier is the most expensive brand on this list, with products ranging from $45 to $95. The Vital Skin Foundation Stick ($68) and Baby Cheeks Blush Stick ($48) are standouts. For luxury beauty consumers transitioning to clean products, Westman Atelier provides a comparable experience to conventional luxury brands like Tom Ford, Charlotte Tilbury, and Pat McGrath.

Best Budget Clean Beauty Options

Clean beauty is not exclusively a premium category. Several brands offer excellent clean formulations at drugstore prices.

  • e.l.f. Cosmetics: e.l.f. has expanded its clean and vegan offerings significantly. The Camo CC Cream SPF 30 ($15), Halo Glow Liquid Filter ($14), and Putty Blush ($8) are all clean-formulated and available at every drugstore.
  • Physicians Formula: Their Organic Wear line is certified organic and priced under $15 for most products. The Organic Wear Tinted Moisturizer ($12) is a standout.
  • Pacifica: Entirely vegan and cruelty-free, Pacifica offers clean makeup and skincare at Target, with most products between $10 and $18.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does clean beauty actually mean?

Clean beauty refers to products formulated without ingredients considered potentially harmful, though there is no universal legal definition. Most clean brands exclude parabens, sulfates, phthalates, synthetic fragrances, formaldehyde, and certain other chemicals. The EU bans over 1,600 cosmetic ingredients while the US FDA bans only about 11, so many clean brands follow EU standards as a baseline. Look for brands with transparent ingredient lists and specific exclusion policies.

Is clean beauty actually better for your skin?

Not automatically. Clean beauty eliminates certain controversial ingredients, but "clean" does not inherently mean more effective. Some conventional ingredients that clean beauty avoids are well-studied and considered safe by dermatologists. The real benefit is ingredient transparency and the avoidance of known irritants. If you have sensitive skin, clean formulations may reduce reactions. For all skin types, focusing on proven actives matters more than whether a product is labeled clean.

Which clean beauty brand has the best foundation?

ILIA Super Serum Skin Tint SPF 40 is widely considered the best clean foundation for light coverage. Kosas Revealer Skin-Improving Foundation SPF 25 is the best for medium-to-full coverage. Both perform comparably to conventional high-end foundations and include skincare benefits.

Are clean beauty products more expensive?

Clean beauty products range from affordable to luxury. Tower 28 and e.l.f.'s clean line offer products under $20. ILIA and Kosas are mid-range at $24 to $48. Westman Atelier is luxury at $45 to $95. The average clean product costs 10 to 20 percent more than conventional equivalents, but drugstore clean options have expanded significantly.

How do I know if a clean beauty brand is legitimate?

Look for specific excluded ingredient lists rather than vague claims. Legitimate clean brands publish full ingredient lists and often hold certifications like EWG Verified, COSMOS Organic, Leaping Bunny, or B Corp. Be skeptical of brands that rely solely on buzzwords without specifics. Check the EWG Skin Deep database to verify ingredient safety ratings.
